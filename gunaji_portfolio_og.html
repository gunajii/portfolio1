03 — Publications

Research Work

Under Review — Conference Submission · 2025

AI Chatbot for Medical Diagnosis and Analysis: A Web Application with a RAG Backend

Co-authors: N. Kadam, V. Shinde · Advisor: Mrs. Snehal Kuche · Available on ResearchGate

  • Designed and implemented a full-stack medical conversational AI using a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipeline, grounding LLM outputs in verified medical knowledge to directly reduce hallucination in high-stakes domains.
  • Built a semantic retrieval engine using ChromaDB and sentence-transformers/all-MiniLM-L6-v2 embeddings, enabling context-aware document retrieval via cosine similarity search.
  • Curated a structured medical knowledge base from WHO, CDC, PubMed Central, and peer-reviewed sources — chunked into ~500-token segments with overlap for retrieval precision.
  • Deployed quantized Llama-2-7B-Chat as the generation backbone, balancing computational efficiency with response quality on constrained hardware.
  • Achieved 88% response accuracy and 3–4 second average latency; validated through functional testing and user evaluation with 15 participants.
  • Demonstrated that RAG architecture meaningfully improves reliability of AI-generated medical information versus ungrounded LLMs — with implications for healthcare accessibility at scale.
